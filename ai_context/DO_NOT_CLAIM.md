# Do Not Claim — Paper 0

1. **Do not claim Paper 0 derives anything.** It is the ontology paper. Derivations are in Papers 1-8 and 13.

2. **Do not claim the four-layer hierarchy is a theorem.** It is an ontological framing.

3. **Do not claim Paper 0 makes numerical predictions.** Numbers (61, 102, 42, $H_0$, $\Omega$ values) are transcribed from downstream.

4. **Do not claim empty Book II chapters mean failed content.** They are placeholders that expand in place as the corresponding technical paper matures.

5. **Do not cite Paper 0 as a proof of any downstream theorem.** Cite the actual proof location (Paper 1 Supp §X, Paper 4 Supp §T_field, etc).

6. **Do not claim the three-layer-reading (v3.1) is independent of the T_ACC framework.** It is a reading axis on Regime R; the T_ACC record is the backing structure.

7. **Do not claim Paper 0 contains the ontological proof of admissibility.** Admissibility is defined; its necessity is an ontological commitment.

8. **Do not conflate the four ontological layers with the six regime projections (π).** They address different axes — ontological layer is about level of abstraction; π is about reading the ACC record in a physical regime.

9. **Do not claim Book III Ch 15 (Open Problems) is exhaustive.** It aggregates known open problems as of the paper's last revision.

10. **Do not claim Paper 0 is a prerequisite for every other paper.** Papers 1-8 and 13 are self-contained derivations; Paper 0 frames them ontologically.

11. **Do not claim the descriptive-framing discipline is the same as formalisation.** Sentence-level descriptive framing is a stylistic commitment; formal content is in the technical papers.

12. **Do not claim philosophical-positioning (Book III Ch 16) sections have empirical content.** They position APF relative to other programmes; empirical claims live in Papers 4/6/8.
